Project Overview
A single channel relay driver module PCB designed as Day 15 of the 30-Day PCB Design Challenge. The board provides a complete, isolated switching interface between a low-voltage microcontroller GPIO and a high-voltage or high-current load such as mains AC equipment, DC motors, or high-power lamps.
The design centers on an NPN transistor driving a 5V relay coil, with a flyback diode for inductive spike suppression, an optocoupler for galvanic isolation between the control side and the load side, and onboard status LEDs for visual feedback. The module is designed to be driven directly from any 3.3V or 5V GPIO without additional external components.

Working Principle
A relay is an electromechanical switch. When sufficient current flows through its coil, it generates a magnetic field that physically moves a set of contacts from their default (normally open or normally closed) position to the actuated position.
The problem with driving a relay directly from a microcontroller GPIO is twofold:

A relay coil draws 50 mA to 90 mA, far exceeding the 8 mA to 40 mA source limit of most MCU GPIO pins.
When the coil is de-energized, the collapsing magnetic field generates a large reverse voltage spike (back-EMF) that can permanently damage the driving transistor or MCU.

This board solves both problems with the following driver chain:
GPIO (SIG) --> Optocoupler Input LED --> Optocoupler Phototransistor
                                                  |
                                             Base of Q1 (NPN BC547)
                                                  |
                                          Collector --> Relay Coil --> VCC
                                          Emitter  --> GND
                                                  |
                                      D1 (1N4007 flyback across coil)
The optocoupler provides complete galvanic isolation — the MCU ground and the relay/load ground are electrically separate, preventing ground loops and protecting the microcontroller from noise generated by inductive load switching.
The flyback diode D1 is placed in reverse bias across the relay coil. When the coil is switched off, the back-EMF spike forward biases D1, creating a safe recirculation path that clamps the spike and dissipates it as heat rather than allowing it to damage Q1.

Relay Contact Configuration
A standard SPDT relay provides three contact terminals:
COM  -- Common terminal (always connected)
NO   -- Normally Open (open when coil is de-energized, closed when energized)
NC   -- Normally Closed (closed when coil is de-energized, open when energized)
All three terminals are broken out to a screw terminal block on this board for maximum flexibility.

Design Notes

The PC817 optocoupler provides galvanic isolation between the MCU control ground and the relay switching ground. This is critical when switching mains-connected loads to prevent ground potential differences from damaging the microcontroller.
R2 (470 Ohm) limits current through the optocoupler input LED to approximately 6 mA on a 3.3V signal, which is within the PC817 operating range. Verify the current transfer ratio (CTR) in the datasheet at this drive current.
D1 (1N4007) must be placed as physically close to the relay coil pins as possible on the PCB. A long trace between the flyback diode and the coil adds inductance that reduces the diode's effectiveness at clamping back-EMF.
The load screw terminal J2 uses a 5.08mm pitch to accommodate the wire gauges typically used with 10A loads. Do not substitute a 2.54mm pitch header for this connector.
Copper pour or wide traces (minimum 1.5mm) should be used on the load-side traces between J2 and the relay contact pins to handle the rated current without excessive resistive heating.
Maintain adequate PCB clearance between the control-side traces and load-side traces per IPC-2221 standards. For 250V AC, a minimum of 1.5mm clearance is required on the PCB surface.
The relay coil and load switching generate significant electromagnetic interference. Place decoupling capacitors (100 nF ceramic) on the VCC line close to U1 and Q1 to suppress noise on the control supply rail.


Safety Notice
This module is designed to switch high-voltage AC or high-current DC loads. When assembling and testing with mains voltage, observe all relevant electrical safety precautions. Ensure the PCB enclosure is non-conductive and that all high-voltage nodes are inaccessible during operation. This design is intended for educational and prototyping purposes.
